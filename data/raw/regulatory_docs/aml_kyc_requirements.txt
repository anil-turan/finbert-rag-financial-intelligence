Anti-Money Laundering (AML) and Know Your Customer (KYC) Requirements
UK Money Laundering Regulations 2017 (as amended) — Summary

OVERVIEW

The Money Laundering, Terrorist Financing and Transfer of Funds (Information on the Payer) Regulations 2017 (MLRs) implement the EU's Fourth Anti-Money Laundering Directive in the UK. Following Brexit, the UK has retained and amended these regulations under the Proceeds of Crime Act 2002 (POCA).

CUSTOMER DUE DILIGENCE (CDD)

Firms must apply CDD measures when:
- Establishing a business relationship
- Carrying out an occasional transaction above €15,000 (or equivalent)
- There is a suspicion of money laundering or terrorist financing
- There are doubts about the accuracy of previously obtained identification information

Standard CDD measures include:
1. Identifying the customer and verifying their identity using documents, data or information from a reliable, independent source
2. Identifying the beneficial owner (any person owning or controlling more than 25% of the entity) and taking reasonable measures to verify their identity
3. Assessing and understanding the purpose and intended nature of the business relationship

ENHANCED DUE DILIGENCE (EDD)

EDD must be applied in high-risk situations, including:
- Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs) and their family members and close associates
- Customers from high-risk third countries (as listed by FATF or the European Commission)
- Complex or unusually large transactions with no apparent lawful purpose
- Correspondent banking relationships

EDD measures include obtaining additional information about the customer and the nature of the relationship, obtaining senior management approval, and conducting enhanced ongoing monitoring.

SIMPLIFIED DUE DILIGENCE (SDD)

SDD may be applied where there is a low risk of money laundering, such as with regulated financial institutions in low-risk jurisdictions, listed companies, government entities, and certain financial products with limited transaction features.

ONGOING MONITORING

Firms must conduct ongoing monitoring of business relationships including:
- Scrutiny of transactions to ensure consistency with the firm's knowledge of the customer
- Keeping documents, data and information up to date
- Identifying and scrutinising transactions that are complex, unusually large, or have no apparent economic purpose

SUSPICIOUS ACTIVITY REPORTS (SARs)

Firms must submit a SAR to the National Crime Agency (NCA) via the SAR Online system where they know, suspect or have reasonable grounds to suspect that a person is engaged in or attempting money laundering or terrorist financing. The firm must not tip off the customer that a SAR has been submitted (tipping-off offence under POCA 2002, s.333A).

RECORD KEEPING

All CDD records, supporting evidence, and transaction records must be retained for a minimum of five years following the end of the business relationship or the completion of the transaction.

RISK-BASED APPROACH

The MLRs require firms to take a risk-based approach to AML. Firms must:
- Assess the ML/TF risks to which the firm is exposed
- Implement appropriate policies, procedures and controls proportionate to the identified risks
- Document and regularly review their risk assessment
- Train staff on AML obligations and firm-specific procedures
