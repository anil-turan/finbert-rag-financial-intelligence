FCA Conduct Rules — Summary for Regulated Firms
Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) — COCON Sourcebook

THE INDIVIDUAL CONDUCT RULES

Rule 1: Act with integrity
You must act with integrity at all times. This means you must be honest and straightforward in all your dealings. You must not deliberately mislead clients, the FCA or any other person about any matter relevant to your regulated activities.

Rule 2: Act with due skill, care and diligence
You must act with due skill, care and diligence in carrying out your functions. This includes keeping your knowledge and skills up to date and performing your role to a competent standard.

Rule 3: Be open and cooperative with the FCA and other regulators
You must deal with the FCA and other regulators in an open and cooperative way. You must disclose appropriately any information that the FCA would reasonably expect notice of. You must not attempt to conceal information from regulators.

Rule 4: Pay due regard to the interests of customers and treat them fairly
You must pay due regard to the interests of customers. This is closely aligned with the FCA's Principle 6 (treating customers fairly — TCF). You must not take advantage of customers' lack of knowledge or put your own interests ahead of customers' interests.

Rule 5: Observe proper standards of market conduct
You must observe proper standards of market conduct. You must not engage in market abuse, manipulate prices or spread false rumours. You must comply with relevant market rules and practices.

SENIOR MANAGER CONDUCT RULES (SC)

SC1: Taking reasonable steps to ensure the business, for which you are responsible, is controlled effectively
Senior managers must take reasonable steps to ensure their area of responsibility has adequate systems and controls. This includes establishing clear lines of responsibility, maintaining appropriate oversight, and responding promptly to control failures.

SC2: Taking reasonable steps to ensure the business for which you are responsible complies with relevant requirements and standards
Senior managers must ensure compliance with all applicable regulatory requirements. This includes FCA rules, PRA rules, and applicable legislation such as the Financial Services and Markets Act 2000 (FSMA).

SC3: Taking reasonable steps to ensure that any delegation of responsibilities is to an appropriate person and that you oversee the discharge of the delegated responsibility effectively
Senior managers remain responsible for areas they delegate. They must select appropriate delegates, provide clear mandates, and monitor performance.

SC4: Disclosing appropriately any information of which the FCA or PRA would reasonably expect notice
Senior managers must ensure timely and complete disclosure to regulators. Self-reporting of material issues is expected.

THE CONSUMER DUTY (PS22/9)

The FCA's Consumer Duty, effective July 2023, sets higher and clearer standards of consumer protection across financial services. Under the Duty, firms must deliver good outcomes for retail customers across four key areas:

1. Products and services: Products must be designed to meet the needs, characteristics and objectives of the target market.
2. Price and value: Firms must offer fair value — the price charged must be reasonable relative to the benefits received.
3. Consumer understanding: Communications must support consumers in making informed decisions.
4. Consumer support: Firms must provide support that meets consumers' needs throughout the product lifecycle.

APPROVED PERSONS REGIME AND SENIOR MANAGERS & CERTIFICATION REGIME (SM&CR)

The SM&CR replaced the Approved Persons Regime for most firms. Under SM&CR:
- Senior Managers require FCA/PRA pre-approval before taking up their roles
- Certified persons must be certified as fit and proper by their firm annually
- All staff are subject to the Conduct Rules

Firms must maintain a Management Responsibilities Map (MRM) and Statements of Responsibilities (SoR) for each Senior Manager.
